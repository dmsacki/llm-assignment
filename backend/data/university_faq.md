## Course Registration
- Course registration at UDSM is done through ARIS 3 (Academic Registration Information System), accessible at aris3.udsm.ac.tz.
- Students log in with their registration number and password, then use the "Course Registration" section to select courses for the current academic year and semester.
- Students should verify course codes and prerequisites carefully before submitting, since errors can affect enrollment.
- A confirmation/registration slip is generated after submission and should be kept for records.

## Examination Rules
- The General Examination Regulations and Student By-Laws govern the conduct of examinations and student discipline at UDSM.
- A student who misses an examination for a genuine reason (e.g. illness, certified by a medical officer) may, with special permission from the Senate or the relevant College/School/Institute Board, sit the examination at a later fixed time.
- Students who fail one or more examinations but whose overall GPA is 2.7 or above are normally eligible to sit supplementary examinations in the failed course(s).
- Any student found to have cheated or engaged in malpractice during an examination is considered to have committed an examination offense, handled under the university's disciplinary procedures.

## Library Services
- The UDSM Library operates from two main buildings: the Dr. Wilbert K. Chagula Library and the University of Dar es Salaam Library, housing different collections (e.g. the East-Africana collection is in the Chagula building).
- Registered students can borrow print materials from general shelves for a set loan period; special reserve and East-Africana collections are typically restricted to staff, postgraduates, and final-year undergraduates.
- The library offers carrel (private study) rooms, bookable online, in two daily sessions (8:00 a.m.–1:00 p.m. and 1:00 p.m.–6:00 p.m.), for a small fee of around TZS 10,000 per session.
- Inter-library loan lets students access materials from partner institutions such as MUHAS and SUA.
- The Reference Desk on the first floor of the library building is the main contact point for help locating resources; library staff can also be reached by email.

## ICT Support
- The Directorate of ICT (DICT) manages UDSM's ICT infrastructure, policies, and support services, including internet access, online learning platforms, and software resources.
- DICT maintains an ICT Policy and a Guideline for ICT Service Management governing acceptable use, data security, and responsible digital practice across campus.
- Most student-facing systems (course registration, LMS, fee payment) are accessed through the central ARIS 3 / Access Portal using single sign-on, reducing the number of separate logins needed.
- For account or system issues, students should contact their college's ICT support point or the central ICT helpdesk channels listed on the DICT pages of udsm.ac.tz.

## Hostel Application
- On-campus accommodation is coordinated by the University Student Accommodation Bureau (USAB), based near Hall V at the Main Campus.
- UDSM has 7 traditional Halls of Residence on the main campus (around TZS 800 per day per bed for undergraduates), plus additional hostels such as the John Pombe Joseph Magufuli Hostels, Mabibo Hostels, Kijitonyama (CoICT) Hostels, and Ubungo Hostel.
- CoICT Hostels (Kijitonyama): Typically charge around TZS 1,000–1,500 per day per bed for undergraduates; postgraduate rates may be higher. Prices are subject to change; confirm current rates with USAB.
- Charges vary by hostel and student category — e.g. Magufuli Hostels charge roughly TZS 500, TZS 2,000, and $3 per day per bed for undergraduate, postgraduate, and international students respectively.
- Students unable to secure on-campus accommodation can get help finding affordable off-campus housing through USAB's partnerships with nearby providers.
- Shuttle transport is available between campus and some hostels (e.g. Mabibo) for off-campus residents.
- For the most current hostel rates and availability, visit USAB or use the ARIS 3 accommodation portal (aris3.udsm.ac.tz).

## Fee Payment
- Tuition and other fees can be paid through the ARIS 3 portal (aris3.udsm.ac.tz), which supports mobile money payments (M-Pesa, Tigo Pesa, Airtel Money) as well as bank transfers.
- Students must show evidence of sponsorship (government or other organization) at the start of each academic year, or pay the full tuition/fees themselves before being permitted to use university facilities for that term.
- Local fee payments should be made by banker's cheque payable to the University Bursar or another Bursar-approved method, and every payment must be receipted.
- ARIS 3 can be used to pay various fee types beyond tuition, including accommodation, ID card, transcript, registration, and examination fees.

## Academic Calendar
- The UDSM academic year is organized into two semesters.
- Each academic unit publishes specific dates for registration, teaching, examinations, and breaks within that structure, available via the University Prospectus and the ARIS 3 / LMS academic calendar pages.
- Students should check their college/school's specific almanac each year, since exact dates can shift annually.

## Student Conduct
- Student behavior at UDSM is governed by the University of Dar es Salaam Student By-Laws (revised 2021), made under the Universities Act No. 7 of 2005.
- The By-Laws define disciplinary offenses and set out procedures for disciplinary hearings and appeals.
- Hall/hostel residents are subject to specific rules, such as a curfew (guests generally not permitted to remain in halls of residence past midnight).
- Disciplinary matters are generally handled through the Dean of Students' office and hall Wardens, with serious cases proceeding to formal university disciplinary bodies.
